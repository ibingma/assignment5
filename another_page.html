The Design Process

Layout

Planning the page layout was the most time-consuming part of the process, as I didn’t have a clear starting point beyond the goal of creating a personal website. I began by searching for potential photos and used Figma to design the layout. After numerous iterations, the final results—some of which are displayed on the right—started to take shape. Ultimately, I selected the design featured on the main page because it felt the most personal and aligned perfectly with the site’s purpose.

Initially, I considered using a full background image, but the subject was too large and distracting from the main text. Instead, I opted for an image on the left and text on the right, as this balanced the elements better. To improve readability, I aligned the content in a grid-like format. I used the float system for positioning the image, text, footer sections, navigation bar with the exception of the header.

Color Palettes

The color palettes used across these webpages were selected from the main page's self-image photo. It was a challenging process, involving a lot of trial and error, as there was no strict rule on which colors to choose or where they should be applied. Ultimately, the colors were picked based on their dominance and contrast, while ensuring they complemented both the image and the overall aesthetic of the page. The goal was to create a visually harmonious and balanced design.

Typography

I chose the Roboto font, designed by Christian Robertson, for the website's typography. Its friendly and open curves create a welcoming feel, which I find ideal for a personal website. Since the purpose of the site is simply to introduce myself—without conveying any critical information—I wanted the reading experience to feel natural and relaxed, complementing the overall design.
